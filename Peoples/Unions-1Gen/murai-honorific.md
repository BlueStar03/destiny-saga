# Murai Honorifics

---


### General, strangers, and behavior

| Suffix     | Explanation                                                                                                 |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **-san**   | Polite adult (or unknown-age person). Neutral to positive default.                                          |
| **-xan**   | Polite child / minor, well-behaved or neutral.                                                              |
| **-dor**   | Stranger / unknown person (no relationship established).                                                    |
| **-dil**   | Inferior / subordinate / lesser. Technically proper but usually considered rude; many use **-san** instead. |
| **-ban**   | “Bad” adult (informal, subjective negative judgment).                                                       |

Here’s the full list of Murai honorifics you’ve defined so far, grouped by function. I’ll keep descriptions short so it’s usable as a reference sheet.

---

## 1. General / Behavioral

- **-san** – Polite adult (or unknown-age person). Neutral to positive.
    
- **-xan** – Polite child / minor, well-behaved or neutral.
    
- **-dor** – Stranger / unknown person. No relationship yet.
    
- **-dil** – Subordinate / inferior. Proper but cold or rude; most people just use **-san** instead.
    
- **-ban** – “Bad” adult (informal, subjective judgment).
    
- **-min** – “Bad” child (informal, subjective judgment).
    
- **-banim** – Convicted criminal (from conviction until 5 years after sentence; can be used longer by choice).
    

---

## 2. Romance / Like-Family

- **-tan** – Like-family friend, close friend, “buddy / best friend.” No kinship, but emotionally family.
    
- **-kun** – Romantic partner (girlfriend/boyfriend/spouse). Affectionate; appropriate in familial settings, rude in formal ones.
    
- **-lin** – Wedded spouse. Can only be used after the wedding ceremony.
    

---

## 3. Family / Kinship

### Generic family

- **-tem** – Generic “you are family to me,” any generation, any branch.
    

### Older generations (before me)

- **-tam** – Prior generation in my family: parents’ generation and above (aunts, uncles, grandparents, great-grandparents, etc.).
    
- **-tamo** – My parents.
    
- **-tama** – My grandparents.
    

### My generation

- **-tun** – My generation in the family: siblings, cousins, etc.
    
- **-tunu** – My sibling (neutral).
    
- **-tuno** – My eldest sibling (respect/affection; can still be called **-tunu**).
    
- **-tuni** – My youngest sibling (respect/affection; can still be called **-tunu**).
    

### Younger generations (after me)

- **-tin** – Next generations in my family: children, grandchildren, nieces/nephews, cousins’ children, etc.
    
- **-tini** – My child.
    
- **-tina** – My eldest child (respect/affection; can also be **-tini**).
    
- **-tinina** – My youngest child / my baby (respect/affection; can also be **-tini**).
    
- **-tino** – My grandchild.
    

---

## 4. Mentorship / Learning / Religious

- **-ra** – Someone I learned from.
    
- **-re** – Someone I learned with (peer learner).
    
- **-ri** – Someone who learned from me.
    
- **-raidan**
    
    - Familial/subjective: someone I aspire to be like, my ideal.
        
    - Formal: a recognized master / expert in their field.
        

---

## 5. Authority / Governance / Status

- **-dan** – My superior (boss, leader, teacher, respected elder).
    
- **-dara** – Holder of an office of governance: chief, mayor, governor, judge; informally also other officials.
    
- **-dairan** – Head of state (king, emperor, president, etc.).
    
- **-dairani** – _My_ sovereign, the ruler I am subject to.
    

---

## 6. Military – Structure, Rank, Allegiance

### Allegiance / type

- **-sor** – Any military person (friend or foe, unknown allegiance).
    
- **-sar** – Friendly / allied military, own-side forces.
    
- **-surin** – Enemy military / hostile combatant.
    

### Ranks

- **-sori** – Soldier (lowest rank).
    
- **-sore** – Warrior (second rank).
    
- **-soro** – Knight (third rank).
    
- **-sora** – Noble rank (fourth rank).
    
- **-sorain** – Archnoble / Supreme Commander; also used for the mission lead whose orders define _my_ mission.
    

### Relational (inside the military)

- **-sir** – Military superior (they outrank me).
    
- **-ser** – Military equal (same rank as me).
    
- **-sur** – Military inferior (I outrank them).
    

---

## 7. Animals

- **-pet** – Animal I own / am responsible for (working animal, mount, etc.).
    
- **-pit** – Beloved companion animal, “pet” in the emotional sense.
